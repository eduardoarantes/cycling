Workout Nutrition

Before Training (1-2 hours before)

  • Easy/Recovery Rides: Normal meal, no special fueling needed
  • Hard Intervals: 50-100g carbs (oatmeal, banana, toast)
  • Long Rides: 100-150g carbs + protein

During Training

  • <60 minutes: Water only
  • 60-90 minutes: 30-60g carbs per hour (sports drink, gels)
  • >90 minutes: 60-90g carbs per hour + electrolytes
  • >3 hours: Add protein (4:1 carb:protein ratio)

After Training (within 30-60 minutes)

All rides: Protein + carbs

Target: 20-30g protein + 60-100g carbs

Examples: Protein shake with banana, chicken with rice, recovery drink

Hydration

  • Daily Baseline: 2-3 liters water minimum
  • During Training: 500-750ml per hour
  • Add electrolytes for: >90min rides
  • Hot weather: increase to 750-1000ml/hour

3. Recovery Strategies

Age 51+ Recovery Considerations

At 51, you need to prioritize recovery more than younger athletes. Muscle damage takes longer to repair, and hormonal profiles (lower testosterone) slow recovery.

Recovery Hierarchy (Most to Least Important)

1. Sleep (Priority #1)

  • Target: 7-9 hours per night
  • Quality matters: dark room, cool temperature, consistent schedule
  • Naps: 20-30 minute power naps can enhance recovery
  • Sleep is when growth hormone is released - critical for adaptation

2. Nutrition

  • Post-workout meal within 60 minutes
  • Adequate daily protein (134-168g)
  • Sufficient carbs to replenish glycogen
  • Anti-inflammatory foods: berries, fatty fish, leafy greens

3. Stress Management

  • Training is stress - life stress impacts recovery
  • Practice: meditation, deep breathing, gentle yoga
  • Work-life balance affects training performance

4. Active Recovery

  • Easy spinning (Z1) promotes blood flow and recovery
  • Better than complete rest for most athletes
  • 30-60 minutes very easy

4. Injury Prevention

Common Cycling Injuries and Prevention

1. Knee Pain

Causes: Improper bike fit, too much volume too soon, weak glutes

Prevention:

  • Professional bike fit
  • Gradual volume increases
  • Strength training (squats, lunges)
  • Proper cleat position

2. Lower Back Pain

Causes: Core weakness, poor bike fit, inflexibility

Prevention:

  • Core strengthening exercises
  • Hip flexor stretching
  • Proper saddle height and handlebar position
  • Break up long rides with standing/stretching

3. Neck/Shoulder Pain

Causes: Aggressive position, tension, weak upper body

Prevention:

  • Upper body strength work
  • Handlebar height adjustment
  • Regular position changes on bike
  • Shoulder stretches

6. Mental Performance

Goal Setting

SMART Goals for Next 12 Weeks

  • Specific: Increase FTP from 260.0W to 275.0W
  • Measurable: FTP test at week 0 and week 12
  • Achievable: 5.8% improvement is realistic
  • Relevant: Aligns with competitive cycling goals
  • Time-bound: 12-week plan with clear timeline

Process Goals (Focus on these weekly)

  • Complete 95% of scheduled workouts
  • Hit power targets within ±5W on intervals
  • Get 7+ hours sleep per night
  • Consume post-workout nutrition within 60 minutes
  • Track resting heart rate daily

Dealing with Setbacks

Missing Workouts

  • 1 missed workout: No makeup needed, continue plan
  • 2+ missed workouts in a week: Repeat that week
  • Week+ missed: Restart from recovery week, adjust timeline

Illness

  • Minor cold (above neck): Easy riding OK
  • Fever, chest cold, body aches: Complete rest
  • Return: Start with 50% volume, gradually increase

Motivation Dips

  • Normal to have low days
  • Focus on process, not outcomes
  • Join group rides for social motivation
  • Remember why you started cycling

Race Day Psychology

Phase Mental Strategy
Pre-Race
  • Trust your training - you've done the work
  • Focus on controllables: nutrition, warm-up, pacing
  • Visualize executing your race plan
During Race
  • Start conservatively - always easier to speed up
  • Monitor power, don't get drawn into early surges
  • Stay present - focus on current effort, not outcome
Post-Race
  • Reflect on what went well
  • Identify 1-2 areas for improvement
  • Celebrate effort regardless of result
