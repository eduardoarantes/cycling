10. Areas for Improvement

1. Limited High-Intensity Training

Issue: Only 7.3% of training time spent in Z4-Z5 (threshold and VO2 max zones).

Impact: FTP development is limited without sufficient time at threshold and above. This is the primary limiter to performance gains.

Solution: Increase Z4-Z5 work to 12-15% of total training time through dedicated threshold intervals (2x20min) and VO2 max sessions (5x5min).

2. Sweet Spot Under-Utilization

Issue: Limited time in sweet spot zone (88-93% FTP), which is highly effective for FTP development.

Impact: Missing out on efficient FTP-building training that's less fatiguing than full threshold work.

Solution: Add 1-2 sweet spot sessions weekly (3x15-20min intervals) during build phases.

3. Power Variability

Issue: Difference between average (170W) and normalized power (163W) suggests some power inconsistency.

Impact: Variable pacing is less efficient than steady-state efforts for building fitness.

Solution: Practice steady-state efforts using ERG mode on trainer. Focus on even pacing in outdoor rides and races.

4. Age-Specific Recovery Management

Issue: At age 51, recovery takes longer than for younger athletes.

Impact: Insufficient recovery can lead to accumulated fatigue, increased injury risk, and diminished training adaptation.

Solution: Mandatory 48-72 hours between hard sessions. Never schedule back-to-back intensity days. Prioritize sleep (7-9 hours) and nutrition (protein {protein_min}-{protein_max}g/day).

5. Periodization Structure

Issue: Training appears consistent month-to-month without clear build/recovery cycles.

Impact: Without periodization, peak performance may be limited and overtraining risk increases.

Solution: Implement 3-week build, 1-week recovery structure with defined Foundation, Build, and Peak phases targeting specific events.

13. Monitoring & Red Flags

Weekly Monitoring

Track These Metrics

  • Resting heart rate (morning, before getting out of bed)
  • Sleep quality and duration
  • Training load (total hours)
  • Intensity distribution (% time in zones)
  • Subjective motivation and energy levels
  • Ability to complete prescribed intervals

Red Flags - Stop Training If:

  • Resting HR elevated 10+ bpm for 3+ consecutive days
  • Unable to complete prescribed intervals at target power
  • Persistent pain (not normal muscle soreness)
  • Sleep disruption lasting 3+ nights
  • Loss of motivation or enjoyment
  • Illness symptoms (fever, persistent cough, etc.)

Action: Take 2-3 days complete rest, then reassess. If symptoms persist beyond 5 days, consult with coach or physician.

Age 51+ Specific Considerations

  • Recovery Time: 48-72 hours between intensity sessions
  • Sleep: 7-9 hours non-negotiable

14. Training Execution Guidelines

How to Execute Intervals

Threshold Intervals (Z4)

Should feel 'comfortably hard' - you can maintain power but conversation is difficult. Focus on steady breathing rhythm. Last 5 minutes should be challenging but sustainable. If you can't complete the full interval, reduce power by 5-10W.

VO2 Max Intervals (Z5)

Very hard effort - breathing is labored. Focus on maintaining power for the full 5 minutes. Recovery between intervals is crucial - spin very easy. It's normal for power to drop slightly in later intervals.

Sweet Spot

Should feel moderately hard but sustainable. You should be able to hold the power for 15-20 minutes consistently. Great 'bang for buck' training - builds FTP without excessive fatigue.

Tempo (Z3)

Moderately hard but conversational with effort. Should feel like a 'solid pace' but not exhausting. Good for race preparation and long-ride preparation.

Indoor vs Outdoor Execution

Best Done Indoors

  • Threshold intervals (2x20)
  • VO2 max intervals
  • Sweet spot intervals
  • Any session requiring precise power control

Can Be Done Outdoors

  • Long endurance rides
  • Recovery rides
  • Tempo work on suitable roads
  • Race simulation (if you have good roads/routes)

Outdoor Execution Tips

  • Find consistent gradients for threshold work (3-5% climbs ideal)
  • Use relatively flat routes for tempo/endurance
  • Avoid heavily trafficked roads for hard intervals
  • Use perceived exertion + heart rate if power is variable
