Red Flags - Stop Training If:

  • Resting HR elevated 10+ bpm for 3+ consecutive days
  • Unable to complete prescribed intervals at target power
  • Persistent pain (not normal muscle soreness)
  • Sleep disruption lasting 3+ nights
  • Loss of motivation or enjoyment

Action: Take 2-3 days complete rest, then reassess. If symptoms persist beyond 5 days, consult with coach or physician.

14. Training Execution Guidelines

How to Execute Intervals

Threshold Intervals (Z4)

Should feel 'comfortably hard' - you can maintain power but conversation is difficult. Focus on steady breathing rhythm. Last 5 minutes should be challenging but sustainable. If you can't complete the full interval, reduce power by 5-10W.

VO2 Max Intervals (Z5)

Very hard effort - breathing is labored. Focus on maintaining power for the full 5 minutes. Recovery between intervals is crucial - spin very easy. It's normal for power to drop slightly in later intervals.

Sweet Spot

Should feel moderately hard but sustainable. You should be able to hold the power for 15-20 minutes consistently. Great 'bang for buck' training - builds FTP without excessive fatigue.

Tempo (Z3)

Moderately hard but conversational with effort. Should feel like a 'solid pace' but not exhausting. Good for race preparation and long-ride preparation.

Indoor vs Outdoor Execution

Best Done Indoors

  • Threshold intervals (2x20)
  • VO2 max intervals
  • Sweet spot intervals
  • Any session requiring precise power control

Can Be Done Outdoors

  • Long endurance rides
  • Recovery rides
  • Tempo work on suitable roads
  • Race simulation (if you have good roads/routes)

Outdoor Execution Tips

  • Find consistent gradients for threshold work (3-5% climbs ideal)
  • Use relatively flat routes for tempo/endurance
  • Avoid heavily trafficked roads for hard intervals
  • Use perceived exertion + heart rate if power is variable
